Communications

Inter– and Itra–agency communications resources

Introduction to RPIA Radio Communications
RPIA utilizes a radio system that allows for dispatching from the Rensselaer County Emergency Communications Center, located in Troy, New York. If an ambulance call is called in to RPI Public Safety, they contact the county’s dispatching center to send us to the scene. We also have our own radio system for use during events/standby coverage, as well as organizing crews to respond to day calls. When hailing others, call their identifier, then yours, wait for them to respond, then proceed with your message.

The "800"
Dispatch and some other emergency services such as ALS intercepts are contacted via the “800" . There is one mobile unit in the ambulance, and three portables – one at the office, one in the ambulance, in order to always have a portable available for crews (specifically during day calls), and one in the first response vehicle. The 800 system is broken up in to channels or “talk groups”, based on function and geography. The important ones are as follows:
  • 1-1 Dispatch: Communication with EMS dispatcher
  • 1-2 Amb to Amb: Ambulance to ambulance communication; used to directly contact another unit, such as an ALS intercept
The 800 system relies on a series of repeater towers to transmit messages. Your transmission is picked up by the nearest tower, and then relayed to dispatch. If there is no available tower, you may receive a “No Access” error on the mobile. Try retransmitting, and then try turning off the radio and turning it back on again if that doesn’t work. If you hit the red emergency activation button on any 800 unit, the unit will automatically switch over to Channel 10, and you receive a dedicated communications line to dispatch; all other communications are blocked out. Power off the unit to clear the emergency activation, and contact the dispatcher (over the air or landline preferable) to inform them of any “accidental trips”.

High Band, RPIA Radios, BLS Radios (VHF)
All other communication is done over the RPIA channels (“high-band radio”, “BLS radio”) using RPIA portables, mobiles or base stations. The RPI Ambulance frequency is repeated from an old Troy Fire frequency (460.xxx) from the county. A repeater on the Walker Building retransmits on our frequency (155.220).
  • Channel 1: RPI Ambulance, 155.220
  • Channel 2: RPI Ambulance, Alert / Pager Mode (will only activate upon receiving pager or call tones)
  • Channel 3: RPI Parking and Transportation Dept.
  • Channel 4: Houston Field House Operations
  • Channel 5: RPI Dept. of Public Safety
  • Channel 6: BLS 340, 155.340 (hospital radio frequency for Albany hospitals)
  • Channel 7: BLS 400, 155.400 (hospital radio frequency for Troy and Schenectady hospitals)
  • Channel 8: EMS 715, 155.715 (statewide, inter-agency operations frequency: all emergency units equipped to use this channel)
  • Channel 9: Empire Ambulance dispatch
  • Channel 10: Mohawk Ambulance dispatch
  • Channel 11: Weather
As a rule of thumb: Drivers talk to county, CC’s talk to hospitals. (Only drivers, crew chiefs, line officers or supervisors should talk to county. Drivers may need to talk to the hospital and vice versa).
