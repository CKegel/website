Radio Callsigns

Intra-agency Use

On RPI frequencies, RPIA uses call signs in the 900-999 range. RPI Public Safety uses 100-199 (Vehicles), 200-299 (Officers), 300-399 (Sergeants), 700-799 (Lieutenants), and 801 and 802 for the Chief or Assistant Chief. RPI Transportation uses the 500-599 and 600-699 range. [FACT CHECK] Presently members who are Crew Chiefs, Drivers, or in active training are given a 900 number between 920 and 990. Once these numbers are assigned, they stay with the member until graduation. The numbers 99x are reserved for on-duty observers/attendants without their unique 900 number. 90x numbers are used to identify crews during special events. 91x numbers are specialty units, such as ALS, who may be present at an event.

Inter-agency use

On any other frequency, RPI Personnel are to identify themselves as:

  • 5939, when representing the ambulance as a crew,
  • “RPI Ambulance Car x”, if they have a car number, or
  • “RPI Driver xx” or “RPI EMT xx” (where xx corresponds to their 9xx number)
These designations are used to be in compliance with standards of dispatch and the rest of Rensselaer County. Every fire and ambulance station in the county has a 2-digit station identifier (RPI Ambulance is Station 59), and each unit a 4-digit identifier consisting of the station and unit number, e.g. 5939 (Ambulance 39, or A-39, was the past identifier for the ambulance). Rensselaer County EMS Vehicle Identifiers

History

According to John Crawford, between an indeterminate date and circa 2004 [FACT CHECK], RPIA used 9xx numbers. At that time, RPIA relied on Nextel phones and text message dispatches. Due to an unacceptably long response time, RPIA switched to the use of Motorola HT-750s which could be used to communicate with county dispatch. Rensselaer County interfaces with many agencies and only recognizes vehicle identifiers (5939), car numbers, or Driver/EMT numbers. Because the county would not recognize the 9xx identifier, the use of this system was discontinued. At this time all members were issued car numbers. Car numbers were issued every year, starting at 1 and counting up sequentially. Line side officers, then Civil side officers were issued car numbers, and the rest issued by seniority. In the fall of 2009, then Captain Peter Ragone ordered the re-use of the 9xx identifier within the agency. This was done for three reasons:
  • To decrease potential confusion when operating with Public Safety or other school entities.
  • Car numbers are only given to line officers in other agencies, and RPI having upwards of 20 cars appeared unprofessional.
  • Car numbers were reissued every year and required members to re-learn identifiers. Constant 9xx numbers would reduce this confusion.
