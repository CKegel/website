Driver Training

The Driver is in charge of the safe operation of the Ambulance and the safe transportation of the crew and patient on calls. Attendants may train to become a Driver under the supervision of a qualified Driver Trainer. The following are the minimum promotional requirements for the Driver course:*

Probationary Driver
  • Hold a Valid NYS Class D Drivers License or equivalent for a minimum of six (6) months
  • Be an RPI Ambulance Attendant
  • Attend and pass the written exam for the RPI Ambulance Driver Training Class
  • Hold a CEVO or RPI Ambulance EVDD Certification
  • Complete the Driver Checklist
  • Complete and submit copy of an Ambulance Vehicle Checklist
  • Complete at least five (5) hours of behind the wheel training under the supervision of a Driver Trainer. 
  • Drive a minimum of two (2) Priority-II calls and receive passing evaluations from a trainer. A driver 
    trainer must accompany the trainee in the driver compartment during these calls.
  • Drive a minimum of one (1) Priority-I call and receive passing evaluations from a trainer. A driver 
    trainer must accompany the trainee in the driver compartment during this call. 
  • Drive to each Albany area hospital with a driver trainer to become familiar with routes to these less 
    common destinations. 
  • Pass the Driver Practical Exam administered by a Driver Trainer
  • Request and receive approval by the Promotional Board to be promoted to Probationary Driver
Driver
  • Be a Probationary Driver
  • Drive two (2) calls as a probationary Driver and receive passing evaluations from a Trainer.
  • Request and receive approval by the Promotional Board as defined in SOPs to be promoted to Driver
Driver Trainer
  • Complete at least ten (10) shifts as a Driver or have been a Driver for at least three (3) months while 
    in service. 
  • Drive three (3) EMS calls, at least one (1) of which is Priority-I. Priority requirement may be waived at 
    the discretion of the Training Committee.
  • Teach a driver related course or drill under the supervision of a Driver Trainer. 
  • Teach at least three (3) Driver Checklist items to a trainee under the supervision of a Driver Trainer. 
  • Conduct at least one (1) vehicle training session with a trainee under the supervision of a Driver 
    Trainer. 
  • Request and receive approval by the promotional board to be promoted to Driver Trainer
