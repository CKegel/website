Crew Chief Training

The Crew Chief (“CC”) is operationally in charge of the duty crew. They are responsible for supervising the crew and providing proper patient care. A Probationary Crew Chief is one who may act as the Crew Chief if a Crew Chief Trainer is on the crew. A “full” Crew Chief can complete a crew and manages patient care during a call. A Crew Chief Trainer may supervise Probationary Crew Chiefs during calls, and provide training and classes, as directed by the Crew Chief Training Coordinator. The following training requirements must be completed:

Probationary Crew Chief
  • Be an attendant
  • Hold valid certification as Emergency Medical Technician – Basic or above in New York
  • Complete the online NIMS IS-100, IS-200, IS-700 and IS-800 courses
  • Take and pass the Crew Chief class and written exam
  • Complete PCR writing class
  • Complete the Crew Chief checklist
  • Pass a Crew Chief practical, and submit an evaluation
  • Complete a minimum of two (2) real calls with a Crew Chief Trainer in the patient compartment and 
    receive a passing evaluation for both
  • Request and receive approval by the promotional board for promotion
Crew Chief
  • Must be a Probationary Crew Chief.
  • Must Crew Chief at least two (2) calls, one of which must be a transport, and receive a passing 
    evaluation
  • Must teach at least three (3) Attendant Checklist items to a trainee under the supervision of a Crew 
    Chief.
  • Student teach 1 training drill, and submit an evaluation
  • Request and receive approval by the promotional board for promotion
Crew Chief Trainer
  • Complete at least ten (10) shifts as a Crew Chief or have been a CC for at least three (3) months
    while in service. 
  • Crew Chief three (3) emergency or non- emergency calls
  • Teach at least three (3) Crew Chief Checklist items to a trainee under the supervision of a Crew Chief 
    Trainer. 
  • Teach a training scenario or Crew Chief class to the membership under the supervision of a Crew 
    Chief trainer. 
  • Request and receive approval by the promotional board for promotion

*Please refer to the SOPs for up-to date requirements.
