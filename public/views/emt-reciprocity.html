EMT Certification Reciprocity

This page is up-to-date as of September, 2010. If you have EMT-Basic training in another state, you may receive EMT Certification in New York through the NYS EMS Reciprocity Process. This page is meant to walk you through the Reciprocity Packet [PDF], linked on that page. This packet contains all the information you should need. This page is meant to summarize, not replace, the instructions found on pages 6 and 7 in that packet. Two items of note for RPI students:

  1. There is a $25.00 fee which cannot be paid with cash or a personal check. Instead, purchase a money order from the Post Office in the Game Room on the first floor of the Union.
  2. You must sign Form DOH-2178 “Verification of EMT Certification” in the presence of a licensed Notary Public. There are several here at RPI; a list of of these individuals is available here . Please be polite: call before you show up.

Eligibility

Determine whether or not you are eligible for reciprocity based on the requirements on pages 2-5. The following is a brief summary.

  • You must be at least 18 years old, with valid EMS certification in another state.
  • If you have NREMT certification, or you have been previously certified in New York: Please see the note on Page 3. You will need to take a NY Refresher Course. The EMT refresher course, like the full EMT course, is taught at RPI. The state of New York will pay the refresher fee if you are affiliate with RPI Ambulance.
  • If you received training through the Military, National Park Service or the federal government: please see the note at the top of page 4.
  • If you have a prior criminal conviction: please see the note on Page 4.
  • You MAY NOT receive NYS reciprocity for any certification which was obtained through reciprocity from another state. Please see the note on page 5 for more information.

Relevant Forms

Your reciprocity application must include three forms available in the packet:
  • DOH-2183 New York State EMT Reciprocity
  • DOH-2178 Verification of EMT Certification
  • DOH-2177 EMT Sheet for Reciprocity
This section will discuss the process of filling out each of these forms.

(1) DOH-2183 Application for New York State EMT Reciprocity (page 9)

Fill in the following:
  • Part A. Personal Data. Fill out each section.
  • Part B. Training/Certification. Certification/Registration/License Number is the EMT number assigned to you by your home state. Also include your National Registry number if you have one. Photocopies of your home state EMT card and your CPR card should be included in your packet.
  • Part C. Level of Training. Check where relevant.
  • Part D. Most Recent Certification Information. Name of Institution is the military base, college, hospital, or other institution where you were trained.
  • Part E. You must sign this document in the presence of a licensed Notary Public on RPI’s campus. Please be polite: call before you show up.

(2) DOH -2178 Verification of EMT Certification (page 10)

Fill in your Home State EMT ID Number, your Date of Birth, your Name, and your Social Security Number. The remaining portion of this form must be completed by your home state EMS office. A listing of all the State EMS offices is available on Page 13 in the Reciprocity Packet. Find your home state EMS office, and send them the following:
  • This form (known as DOH-2178),
  • A self addressed, stamped envelope,
  • A letter instructing your home EMS office to complete the form and return it to you. Allow a few weeks for processing.
DO NOT open the envelope when the document is returned to you! In order to prevent forgery, the state of New York requires that this document be sent to them in a sealed envelope.

(3) DOH-2177 EMT Sheet for Reciprocity (page 11)

Detailed information about completing this form is available on Page 8 of the Reciprocity Packet. Complete the top portion of this page. Be sure to print your information in CAPITAL LETTERS.

Other Documents

Three other items are required as a part of your packet: (4 and 5) Copies of your EMT Certification and Current CPR Card If you have not already, photocopy your home state EMT Card and your current CPR card. These items are required by New York State.

(6) Money Order

The current fee for EMT-B reciprocity is $25.00, and $50.00 for higher training levels. Personal checks are not accepted, but you may purchase a Money Order at the Post Office in the Game Room on the first floor of the Rensselaer Student Union.

Completing Your Application (Reciprocity Application Checklist, page 12)

Review this document to ensure that you’ve included all SIX of the required items:
  1. Completed, signed, notarized DOH-2183 New York State EMT Reciprocity.
  2. Sealed Envelope containing DOH-2178 Verification of EMT Certification, completed by your home EMS office.
  3. DOH-2177 EMT Sheet for Reciprocity. Ensure that you’ve filled out this form with CAPITAL LETTERS.
  4. Photocopy of your home state EMT certification card or document. DO NOT send the original.
  5. Photocopy of your CPR card. DO NOT send the original.
  6. Certified check or Money Order for $25.00.
Send these items to: New York State Department of Health Bureau of Emergency Medical Services Reciprocity Unit 433 River Street, Suite 303 Troy, NY 12180 You should recieve your NYS card within a few weeks. When you do, be sure to inform the officers of your EMT number. Good luck!
